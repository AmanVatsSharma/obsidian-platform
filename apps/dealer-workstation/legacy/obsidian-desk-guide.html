Core Principles

The Dealer's Mindset

OBSIDIAN DESK is built around a single truth: a dealer makes hundreds of micro-decisions per hour, each with real financial consequences. Every design choice eliminates friction from those decisions.

⚡
Speed is survival
Orders time out. Prices move. Every second you spend hunting for a button is a second the market has moved against your client. The keyboard shortcuts (A/R/Q) exist because moving a hand to the mouse costs 300ms. Learn them on day one.
🎯
Color communicates danger
You don't read every number on every order. Your eye catches red. Green means safe. Amber means watch. Red means act now. The entire color system is designed around this peripheral vision pattern — trust it.
🧭
Left to right = time pressure
Left rail = now (orders demanding action). Center = context (prices, book, history). Right = control (push quotes, hedge, events). Your attention flows left-to-right throughout the shift.
📊
Book first, client second
A dealer's primary responsibility is the firm's book exposure, not individual client satisfaction. Always check the Book tab's net exposure before accepting large orders — a $2M buy order is great for the client but risky if the book is already heavily long.
🔔
Toasts are urgent, tabs are reference
Toast notifications (top-right) demand immediate attention. Tabs are for when you have a moment to dig deeper. Never ignore a red toast. Never let the surveillance tab accumulate HIGH alerts without reviewing them.
Component 1

Command Bar

The 52px strip at the top. Persistent. Always visible. Contains global system state, session awareness, and the three emergency actions. Learn to read it at a glance.

🔧
Left Zone — Identity & Time
Logo · Dealer name · Shift timer · UTC clock · Session pills
🕐
Shift Timer
Counts up from 00:00:00. Turns amber after 6 hours and red after 8 hours. A red shift timer means you're in overtime — cognitive performance degrades. Escalate to supervisor for handover.
🌍
Session Pills (NY · LON · TKY · SYD)
Green pill + pulsing dot = session open. Grey pill = closed. Why it matters: overlapping sessions (NY+LON 13:00–17:00 UTC) produce the highest liquidity and tightest spreads. Single-session periods may require wider spreads on thin books.
📶
Latency Badge
Round-trip time to the matching engine. Green <10ms (normal). Amber 10–50ms (monitor). Red >50ms (investigate — high latency means prices are stale, clients may arb you). Report to IT immediately if red persists over 30 seconds.
🎛
Middle Zone — Mode Toggles
AUTO-HEDGE · STP mode · B-Book exposure · Margin calls
🤖
AUTO-HEDGE toggle
When ON (green, pulsing): the system automatically hedges net book exposures above the configured threshold at LP1/LP2. Turn OFF during market hours if you want manual control of hedge timing — e.g., before a major event when LP spreads widen significantly.
🔀
STP Mode (3-state toggle)
ON = all orders straight-through to LP (no dealer intervention). MIXED = orders below size threshold auto-STP, large orders require manual review. OFF = full B-Book mode, all orders require dealer decision. Most shifts run MIXED.
💰
B-Book Exposure
Current dollar value of internalized client positions. Amber up to $5M (normal). Red over $5M — you're exceeding the approved B-Book limit. Open the Book tab and route next large orders to A-Book/LP until exposure drops.
🚨
Right Zone — Emergency Buttons
HALT ALL · WIDEN SPREADS · SUSPEND TRADING — all require typed confirmation
⚠
Confirmation Required
All three emergency actions require you to type a confirmation word (HALT / WIDEN / SUSPEND) in a modal. This deliberate friction prevents accidental triggering during a high-stress situation.
⛔
HALT ALL
Immediately freezes all auto-accepts. No new orders are processed automatically. The order queue will still receive orders but all require manual action. Use when: suspecting a platform malfunction, news event creates extreme volatility, or a connectivity issue is detected.
⚡
WIDEN SPREADS
Applies a 3× emergency spread multiplier across all instruments for all clients. Use in the 2–3 minutes before a high-impact event (CPI, FOMC, NFP). The right rail's events panel has a quick per-instrument widen button — prefer that for targeted widening. Use this for platform-wide emergency only.
🔒
SUSPEND TRADING
Disables ALL new order placement across the entire platform. The most drastic action available. Use only when: LP connections are fully down, platform integrity is compromised, or directed by senior management. Clients cannot trade until you click Resume (appears after suspension).

Reading the Card — Key Signals
VIP badge + purple border
High-value client. Exercise extra care. Do not reject without valid reason.
Age timer (1.2s)
Amber at 1–3s. Red at 3s+. A client sees their order as "pending" — this is their frustration clock.
Notional value ($543K)
Gold color = over $500K. Triggers gold left-border. Size affects B-Book risk calculations significantly.
Book impact line
This single line tells you if accepting increases or decreases your book risk. Amber = increases long risk. Blue = reduces exposure.
Slippage preview
+0.2 pip = client will get a slightly better price than they expect (good). Negative slippage = they get worse — consider requoting.
🎨
Left Border Color System
What the colored bar on the left of each card means
Green — New (<1s)
Just arrived. Market price is still fresh. You have time to read the full card.
Amber — Aging (1–3s)
Client is waiting. Price has moved slightly. Decide quickly. Don't read every line again.
Red — Urgent (>3s)
Client sees a very slow response. Card pulses. Price drift may warrant a requote. Act now.
Purple — VIP Client
Overrides age color. High-value account. Rejection risk is a relationship risk. Escalate if unsure.
Gold — Large Notional (>$1M)
Order size materially affects book exposure. Check Book tab before accepting. Consider partial routing to LP.
Grey — Standard Order
Retail client, sub-$500K notional, within normal parameters. Auto-accept may be appropriate.
🔄
Order Decision Framework
The 3-second mental checklist before pressing ACCEPT
1. WHO? → Tier badge (VIP/PRO/RETAIL) + client ID 2. WHAT? → Direction (BUY/SELL) + Symbol + Lots + Notional $ 3. RISK? → Book impact line: does this INCREASE or DECREASE net exposure? 4. PRICE?→ Slippage: positive (favorable) = accept. Negative > 1pip = requote. 5. AGE? → Green = relax. Amber = decide now. Red = requote or reject.
💡
Pro Tip: The Requote
Requoting is not rejection — it's professional practice. When price has moved significantly during an aging order, press Q to inline-requote. The client gets 10 seconds to accept the new price. This protects both parties.
⚠
Bulk Actions Require Confirmation
ACCEPT ALL / REJECT ALL will prompt a browser confirmation before executing. Use bulk reject sparingly — mass rejections for a client group can trigger surveillance alerts and damage relationships.
Component 3

Client Watchlist

The bottom 35% of the left rail. A curated list of high-priority clients whose account health you monitor continuously. Sort order is always margin% ascending — the most at-risk client is always at the top.

Margin LevelStatusVisual CueWhat to Do
>50% NORMAL Green left border No action needed. Monitor periodically.
30–50% MARGIN WARNING Amber border + pulsing row Consider calling the client to add funds or reduce positions. Flag for senior dealer awareness.
<30% MARGIN CALL Red pulsing border + bell Immediate action. Call client + send notification. Auto-close triggers at <20%. Inform risk desk.
Manual SUSPENDED Purple badge Account restricted by compliance/dealer. No orders will be accepted. Contact client only via official channel.
🔴
Auto-Close Threshold
When a client's margin level drops below 20% (configurable in the Risk tab), the system automatically closes their largest losing position. This generates a toast alert and an entry in the Execution log with route "AUTO". You must review these within 2 minutes.
Component 4

Price Board

Six instrument tiles across the top of the center workspace. Prices tick every 700ms from the live feed. This is your market pulse — you should be able to read any tile in under 2 seconds.

📊
Reading a Price Tile
The anatomy of a single instrument tile
▼
BID (left, red label)
The price your client gets when they SELL. Clicking the BID side opens a quick SELL ticket. Last 2 digits are shown larger — that's the pip digit that changes most frequently.
▲
ASK (right, green label)
The price your client pays when they BUY. Clicking the ASK side opens a quick BUY ticket. The spread = ASK − BID. Watch the spread indicator — it widens automatically near events.
📈
Sparkline (30 ticks)
Last 30 price ticks as a line chart. Green line = upward trend. Red line = downward. Gives you directional context in one glance without reading H/L numbers.
⚖
Long/Short Ratio Bar
Shows the percentage split of client positions. Green = long% / Red = short%. When the bar is heavily one-sided, the book is exposed. Check the Book tab if it exceeds 75% in either direction.
🏷
STP / B-Book Badge
Click the badge to toggle routing per instrument. STP green = orders going to LP. B-BK blue = internalized. Toggle individual instruments when doing targeted risk management.
💡
Price Flash
When a price ticks UP, the bid/ask flashes green. DOWN = red flash. Only the changed digit highlights — this peripheral visual cue lets you detect direction without reading the number.
Component 5

6 Workspace Tabs

The lower half of the center workspace. Each tab serves a distinct operational purpose. Know which to open in which situation — switching tabs mid-crisis costs precious seconds.

Tab 1
📚 BOOK
The aggregated position table. Your operational home base. Open this tab whenever you need to understand the firm's net exposure across all instruments before making a large order decision.
  • Net long/short per instrument in lots and $
  • B-Book vs A-Book ratio bars per symbol
  • Risk % gauges (% of limit used)
  • APPLY DELTA HEDGE and FLATTEN BOOK buttons
  • LP routing selector per instrument
Tab 2
⚡ EXECUTIONS
Real-time blotter of every trade that has been processed. New rows slide in from the top with a green flash. Use this to verify an order was filled correctly after accepting it from the queue.
  • Fill price vs market price + slippage in pips
  • Latency color-coded: green/amber/red
  • Route badge: AUTO / MANUAL / STP / TIMEOUT
  • Filter by symbol, route type, time range
  • Aggregation strip: totals, avg slippage, STP ratio
Tab 3
⚠ RISK
Comprehensive risk monitoring center. Open this tab at the start of your shift, before any major event, and whenever a HIGH surveillance alert fires. Check the stress test table before a CPI release.
  • Circular exposure gauges per instrument (0–100% of limit)
  • Clients near margin call, sorted by urgency
  • Top 10 open positions across all clients
  • Tail risk stress scenarios (±50/100/200/500 pip)
  • Inline risk limit configuration
Tab 4
👥 CLIENTS
Full live client table. 28 clients with real-time P&L ticking. Click any row to expand an inline accordion showing all open positions and quick actions (close all, adjust margin, suspend).
  • Sortable/filterable by status, type, equity
  • Live Float P&L cell ticks and flashes
  • Inline accordion: positions + account details
  • CLOSE ALL / ADJUST MARGIN / SUSPEND per client
  • Bulk actions: send message, change group, export
Tab 5
🔍 SURVEILLANCE
Compliance and abuse detection center. A red badge on this tab means HIGH severity alerts are active — do not ignore. Every alert must be acted on (Investigate / Flag / Dismiss / Restrict) before end of shift.
  • Live alert feed: latency arbitrage, news trading, coordination
  • Severity: HIGH (red) / MEDIUM (amber) / LOW (grey)
  • Pattern analysis table with risk scores 0–100
  • Active trading restrictions list with LIFT option
  • Add new restriction: type / duration / reason
Tab 6
💬 CHAT
Internal team communication channel. Channels: #dealing-desk (ops), #risk-alerts (system), #compliance (legal), #general, #breaks. System auto-posts margin call and alert notifications to #risk-alerts.
  • Channel list + unread count badges
  • Direct message to individual team members
  • Presence indicators: online / away / offline
  • Today's event timeline pinned in right sidebar
  • Press Enter to send messages
💡
Tab Priority During Crisis
During a volatile period (e.g., major news event), keep RISK tab open in your mental model. Use keyboard shortcuts to process orders from the left rail without looking at the tab area. Switch to BOOK only when you need to check exposure. Let SURVEILLANCE accumulate during the event, then triage after the initial volatility passes.
Component 6

Right Rail Controls

Three stacked panels: Manual Quote Desk (top), Hedging & LP Panel (middle), Events & News (bottom). This is your control surface — where you actively push quotes, manage hedges, and monitor upcoming market-moving events.

🎯
Manual Quote Desk (Top 35%)
Override market prices for specific clients or all clients
📌
Symbol Selector + Live Prices
Select any of the 6 instruments from the dropdown. The current bid/ask/spread updates in real time from the live feed. All manual price inputs are pre-filled with the current market price.
⚡
Spread Quick Buttons
Five buttons: -0.5 · NORMAL · +0.5 · +2 · +5. Use these before events. A +2 pip pre-event spread prevents the firm taking losses from spike fills. Always click REVERT TO MARKET after the event settles.
📤
PUSH QUOTE TO CLIENTS
The blue button pushes your manually set prices to all selected clients. They will see your price, not the raw market feed. Use the "Affected clients" selector to target Retail only, Pro only, or All. Remember: always REVERT TO MARKET after use.
⚖
Hedging & LP Panel (Middle 35%)
LP connection status · delta exposures · hedge execution
🔌
LP Connection Status
Three LP connections with latency and uptime. Green pulsing dot = connected. Static red dot = disconnected. LP3 (Currenex) is currently down — click RECONNECT to retry. All hedging routes automatically to LP1/LP2 while LP3 is down.
📐
Delta Exposure Rows
Shows the net book exposure per instrument that needs hedging. Amber = net long (clients bought more than sold). Green ~FLAT = balanced book. Each row has a HEDGE button to send a hedge order directly to LP for that specific instrument.
⚡
HEDGE ALL DELTAS
Sends hedge orders for all non-flat instruments simultaneously. Use this after accepting several large orders in sequence, or before a scheduled break. Confirm in the Executions tab that all hedge orders filled correctly.
📅
Events & News (Bottom 30%)
Economic calendar · caution mode · news feed
⚡
CAUTION MODE (amber banner)
Automatically activates 5 minutes before any HIGH-impact event. The amber banner across the top of the right rail is your visual cue. When you see it: widen spreads on affected instruments, check book exposure, inform the team in #dealing-desk chat.
🔴
HIGH impact events (red border)
CPI, NFP, FOMC — these move markets 50–200 pips in seconds. The card shows Previous, Forecast, and countdown timer. The WIDEN button applies a pre-defined spread premium specifically to the most affected instruments.
📰
News Feed sentiment dots
● = bullish for base currency. ● = bearish. ● = neutral. These are heuristic — useful for quick context scanning, not trading decisions.

Playbook

5 Real-World Scenarios

Step-by-step walkthroughs for the most common critical situations a dealer faces. Read these before your first shift, then keep this guide open in a browser tab alongside OBSIDIAN DESK.

1
Large VIP Order Arrives During CPI Release
EUR/USD · $2.1M notional · VIP client · 30 seconds before CPI
CRITICAL ▼

Client Omar Al-Rashid (#5503, VIP) submits a 20-lot SELL EUR/USD at market. The CPI release is in 28 seconds. The right rail shows caution mode active and EUR/USD spread already at +2 pips.

  1. 1
    Check the age timer on the order card. It just arrived (green border, <1s). You have time to think but not much.
  2. 2
    Read the book impact line: SELL 20 lots will REDUCE net LONG exposure. This is actually good for book risk if you're currently long. Confirm in the Book tab's EUR/USD net row.
  3. 3
    Check slippage: −0.4 pip (negative). The client is getting a slightly worse price. At $2.1M notional, this is significant. Press Q to requote at market.
  4. 4
    In the requote panel, the price is pre-filled with current market. Adjust to current mid and set validity to 5 seconds — tight because CPI is imminent. Click SEND REQUOTE.
  5. 5
    Post a quick note in #dealing-desk chat: "Requoting VIP 5503 20-lot SELL EUR/USD — CPI in 25s. Manual review."
  6. 6
    If client accepts the requote: confirm fill in the Executions tab. If they decline: the order expires. Either way, immediately after CPI prints, click REVERT TO MARKET in the quote desk panel.
2
Margin Call — Client Equity Dropping Fast
Retail client · margin level 22% → 18% · open BTC/USD long position
URGENT ▼

Anna Kowalski (#7841, RETAIL) appears in the watchlist with a pulsing red border. BTC/USD is dropping. Her margin has fallen to 22% and is trending toward the 20% auto-close threshold.

  1. 1
    Spot the red pulsing row in the watchlist. Her margin reads 22% — the danger zone is 20% auto-close. You have a narrow window.
  2. 2
    Switch to the Clients tab. Find Anna's row (sort by margin ascending — she'll be near the top). Click her row to expand the accordion.
  3. 3
    In the expanded panel, review her open positions. She holds 0.10 lots BTC/USD long — her only position. Float P&L is −$440.
  4. 4
    Check Risk tab → Clients Near Margin Call panel. Anna should appear at the top. The panel shows her margin in real time.
  5. 5
    Call the client via the [CALL] button in her row. Inform her of the margin situation. Give her 2 minutes to add funds via client portal.
  6. 6
    If she cannot top up, use CLOSE ALL POSITIONS from her accordion. This executes a market order closing her BTC/USD position. A green toast confirms execution.
  7. 7
    Log the action in #risk-alerts chat with timestamp and reason. The Surveillance tab will automatically log the close event.
3
LP3 Disconnects During Active Session
Currenex connection drops · 89 open positions · hedge orders pending
CRITICAL ▼

A red toast fires: "LP3 Currenex connection lost." The status bar updates: LP3 shows a static red dot. Several hedge orders were routing through LP3.

  1. 1
    Don't panic. LP1 and LP2 are still active. The system automatically fails over STP orders to LP1/LP2. No client orders are lost — they route to the next best LP.
  2. 2
    Navigate to the Right Rail → LP STATUS section. Confirm LP1 (LMAX) and LP2 (Integral) show green pulsing dots. Note their latency — may be slightly elevated due to rerouted volume.
  3. 3
    Click the RECONNECT button next to LP3 (Currenex). An info toast fires: "Attempting reconnect…" — wait 30 seconds before trying a second time.
  4. 4
    Open the Book tab. Check if any hedge orders were mid-execution when LP3 dropped. Look for unmatched delta exposures in the delta panel (right rail) — any symbol showing large lots that aren't marked as hedged.
  5. 5
    Use HEDGE ALL DELTAS to re-submit any unhedged positions through LP1/LP2. Confirm fills in the Executions tab (route should show LP1 or LP2).
  6. 6
    Post in #dealing-desk chat: "LP3 Currenex down at [time]. Failover to LP1/LP2 active. Reconnect attempt pending. No client impact. Monitoring."
  7. 7
    If LP3 does not reconnect within 5 minutes, escalate to IT via the break-glass procedure. Do not attempt more than 3 manual reconnects.
4
Book Exposure Hits Risk Limit — EUR/USD
Net long 45 lots → approaching 100-lot limit · gauge turns amber
HEDGING ▼

The EUR/USD gauge in the Risk tab has turned amber (exposure 68% of limit). Several large BUY orders have pushed the net long book to 45.2 lots. More buys are expected given positive EUR sentiment.

  1. 1
    Open the Risk tab. The EUR/USD circular gauge shows 68% — AMBER. At 80% it turns red and triggers an alert. At 100% you're in violation of limits.
  2. 2
    Open the Book tab. EUR/USD row: Long 68.4 lots / Short 23.2 lots = net +45.2 lots long. Current price 1.08452. Dollar exposure: ~$4.9M.
  3. 3
    Decide on hedge size. Common approach: hedge 50% of net (22.6 lots) to reduce exposure to 34% of limit while keeping some B-Book P&L potential.
  4. 4
    Navigate to Right Rail → DELTA EXPOSURE. EUR/USD shows ▲ 45.2 lots. Click HEDGE EUR/USD — this submits a SELL 45.2 lots order to LP1.
  5. 5
    For partial hedge: go to the Book tab → Hedging Controls. Set hedge ratio to 50% for EUR/USD. Click APPLY DELTA HEDGE — sends 22.6 lot hedge to LP1.
  6. 6
    Confirm in the Executions tab that the hedge order filled. Route should show STP and LP column should show LP1.
  7. 7
    Return to Risk tab. EUR/USD gauge should now show ~34% (green zone). Update #dealing-desk: "EUR/USD book partially hedged -22.6 lots via LP1. Exposure now 34% of limit."
5
Surveillance: Latency Arbitrage Detected
HIGH alert · Client filling at 340ms stale prices · repeated pattern

Playbook — Critical

Emergency Procedures

Three escalating levels of emergency response. Each requires typed confirmation. Each has a specific trigger condition. Memorize the trigger conditions — not the button locations.

🚨
All Three Require Typed Confirmation
You must type HALT, WIDEN, or SUSPEND exactly — no copy-paste. This 5-second friction is intentional. In a genuine emergency your hands may be shaking. The typing requirement forces a moment of conscious intent.
⚡
Level 1: WIDEN SPREADS
Type WIDEN to confirm · Reversible in <5 seconds · Affects all clients
When to use: 2–3 minutes before HIGH-impact event (CPI, NFP, FOMC). Extreme market volatility. Flash crash conditions. Thin liquidity (major holiday, overnight session).
What it does: Applies a 3× multiplier to all instrument spreads for all clients. Clients can still trade, just at wider prices. This protects the firm from large slippage fills during volatility. Always revert to normal after 30 minutes maximum.
⛔
Level 2: HALT ALL
Type HALT to confirm · Pauses auto-processing · Manual mode only
When to use: Platform connectivity issues. Suspicion of price feed manipulation. Multiple LP disconnections simultaneously. Unexplained large P&L swings with no market explanation.
What it does: Freezes ALL auto-accepts. The order queue still receives orders but every single one requires manual dealer decision. Clients experience slower execution but can still trade. Lifts automatically on a 30-minute timer or when you click Resume.
🔒
Level 3: SUSPEND TRADING
Type SUSPEND to confirm · Full platform shutdown · Senior approval required
When to use: Total LP failure (all three LPs down). Regulatory emergency. Platform security breach. Direct instruction from senior management, risk director, or compliance.
What it does: Completely disables all new order placement across the entire platform. All clients receive a "Trading Temporarily Unavailable" message. Existing positions remain open and continue to float.
☎
Call your supervisor before triggering Level 3
Suspending the platform without authorization may violate regulatory obligations. Unless there is an immediate financial or security threat, call your shift supervisor first. The 30-second call is worth the protection it provides you.
